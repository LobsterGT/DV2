Education In Malaysia (2000 - 2022)

The government should consider increasing the number of schools in East Malaysia, as the region currently has a significantly lower number of schools compared to Peninsular Malaysia.
Female enrolment has consistently remained higher than male enrolment throughout the entire period from 2000 to 2022, with its highest point occurring in 2004 (91.3%).

The government should introduce programs to encourage more male students to stay in school and pursue higher education, while continuing to support female students' progress.

The government should allocate more financial resources to the education sector to enhance school facilities, teacher training, and access to quality education across all regions.

The government should implement strategies to increase participation in post-secondary education. Strengthening post-secondary opportunities will help balance the student distribution across all educational stages.

The government should consider investing in policies that support 25-34 age group, ensuring continued access to higher education, training programs, and career advancement opportunities.
